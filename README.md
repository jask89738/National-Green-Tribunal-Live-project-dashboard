About the Researcher
Jasleen Kaur is an MBA student specializing in Development and Sustainable Finance. With a keen interest in environmental policy, green jurisprudence, and sustainable business models, this project represents the intersection of law, economics, and ecology in the Indian context.

Project Vision
This live research project aims to bridge the gap between environmental adjudication and on-ground sustainability outcomes. By analyzing the National Green Tribunal's orders through the lens of circular economy principles, the project seeks to offer actionable policy recommendations and raise awareness about India's green governance framework.

Why This Matters
India faces immense environmental challenges — from river pollution and unregulated e-waste to landfill crises and plastic contamination. The NGT stands as a specialized body empowered to enforce environmental norms. Understanding its impact is crucial for shaping future environmental policy and advancing India's sustainability goals aligned with the UN SDGs.

Ethical Note
All NGT orders used in this research are publicly available primary sources. Any interviews conducted follow informed consent protocols, and respondent identities are anonymized where needed. Field visits comply with local safety regulations.
